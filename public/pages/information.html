Study

Lectures

Condition

Registered as a course participant as stated on the presence of the lecturer.

Activity

Things to consider are Student Presence Cards, List of lecturers course participants, Recapitulation of attendance, Students must at least take 75% of lecturer learning.

Taking Courses

  • The number of credits that students may take is limited by the previous SAI (Semester Achievement Index). The maximum credit that can be taken is 24 credits.

  • If the SKS withdrawal is more than the predetermined limit, the system will automatically deduct it. Students can determine courses that are canceled when KRS is on line.

Exam

Score

Score Input

  • The lecturer submits the Original Score of the exam results to the head of the Faculty/Department for examination and is archived by the faculty/department.

  • The value input process is carried out by each lecturer through one of 2 alternatives :

    1. Scores can be filled in by direct input to a file that has been provided on a compact disc (CD) by the Faculty. (Excel format and/or program).

    2. For lecturers who are familiar with the internet, they can input and upload grades online, through the Application Form on the Faculty computer that is connected to the network.

  • For the purpose of uploading grades, each lecturer has a personal User ID and Password provided by the IT Division. For security purposes, the password can be changed by the person concerned.

  • The score uploaded by the Officer is done with the User ID and Password of the Officer.

Score Fix

  • Updating of grades can be done with written permission/approval from the Faculty Leader.

  • Approval letter for value improvement followed up by Officer and archived.

  • The procedure for the proposed value update is as follows:

    • From the students:

      1. Students report to the supervisor.

      2. If the lecturer deems it necessary to revise grades, the lecturer reports to the Head of the Department / Deputy Dean I

      3. The head of the Department / Deputy Dean I issues an approval letter for improving grades and submits it to the Officer

    • From the lecturer

      1. If the lecturer deems it necessary to revise grades, the lecturer reports to the Head of the Department / Deputy Dean I.

      2. The Head of Department / Deputy Dean I issues an approval letter for improving grades and submits it to the Officer.

  • Maximum value update time is 2 weeks after graduation.

  • The mechanism for input and value updates for the Short Semester (SS) and remediation exams is the same as for the Semester Value Input/Update.

  • BAA and IT are unable to update grades.

Score Output

  • During the consultation period and filling out the KRS, the Faculty Officer (administrator) prints out 1 KHS (Study Result Card) and is signed by the Academic Supervisor (AS) for student archives.

  • Semester scores and Interim Transcripts can be viewed via the internet or computers provided by the faculty.

  • The final grade transcript is printed by BAA for graduation or other relevant purposes.

Score Files

  • Faculties/Departments archive original grades and print outs from lecturers, proof of approval for grade updates, and printouts of grades at the end of each semester (KHS) in the form of print outs.

  • Students archive the printed semester scores (KHS) which have been signed by the Academic Supervisor lecturer.

  • BAA and IT Division back up all transaction data files and all values to the hard disk or CD.
